Icon of Paris

Built for the 1889 World's Fair, the Eiffel Tower was originally intended to be a temporary installation. Designed by Gustave Eiffel, it stands as a global symbol of France and one of the most recognizable structures in the world.

Rising to a height of 330 meters, the "Iron Lady" was the tallest man-made structure in the world for 41 years. Today, it remains a masterpiece of iron-lattice engineering and a must-see destination for millions of travelers.

Fascinating Tower Facts

  • The tower is made of 7,300 tons of iron.
  • It actually grows about 15cm in the summer due to thermal expansion.
  • There are 1,665 steps to reach the very top.
  • It is repainted every seven years to prevent rust.
